  • 1. The Westminster Walk


    London is the L.A., D.C., and N.Y.C. of Britain all rolled into one. This walk starts with London's "star" attraction, continues to its "Capitol," passes its answer to the "White House," and ends at its "Times Square."


    Hi, I'm Rick Steves. Thanks for joining me on a walk through Westminster, from Big Ben to Trafalgar Square. As London's political center, the Westminster neighborhood is both historic and contemporary. We'll see the River Thames where London was born, pass statues and monuments from London's past, admire the Halls of Parliament where Britain is ruled today, and take a peek at #10 Downing Street, home of the Prime Minister. Allow about an hour for this walk, as we weave through modern traffic and big-city bustle to survey 2,000 years of fascinating history. Our walk ends at Trafalgar Square, where we stand at the very center of one of the world's great cities - London.
