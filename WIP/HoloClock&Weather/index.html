One of the standout pieces in my collection is the Braun ET 33 calculator manufactured by Braun, a German company known for its high-quality consumer electronics and industrial design. The ET 33 was introduced in the early 1970s and quickly gained popularity for its sleek and minimalist design, which was characteristic of the Braun design ethos influenced by the renowned industrial designer Dieter Rams.


The aim of this experimental side project is to delve into alternative color combinations for a calculator originally released in a single color scheme: a black body with colored buttons and accents.
